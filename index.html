📋 Step-by-step setup

This takes about 5–10 minutes and only needs to be done once. You'll need a Google account.

1Go to console.firebase.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
2Click "Add project", name it kitchen-inventory, disable Google Analytics when asked (not needed), and click Create project.
3In the left sidebar click Firestore Database, then Create database. Choose Start in test mode and pick a server location closest to you (e.g. europe-west2 for UK). Click Enable.
4Back on the project overview page, click the web icon </> to add a web app. Give it the nickname kitchen-app. Do not enable Firebase Hosting. Click Register app.
5You'll see a block of code with your config. Copy the values for apiKey, authDomain, projectId, and appId into the boxes below.

🔑 Your Firebase credentials

Paste the values from the Firebase console here. These are stored only on your device.

📱 Installing on your phone

1Open this file in Chrome on your phone (not Samsung Browser).
2Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in Chrome, then tap "Add to Home screen".
3Tap Add. The app icon will appear on your home screen like a regular app.
4Do the same on your wife's phone — both phones will then share the same data automatically.
